Crime in the United States: An Analysis of Age

The chart above shows various different kinds of offenses committed in the United States last year, 2016. The bars show age ranges for the population, and the corresponding arrests can be compared and contrasted among the different age groups. It is important to be able to recognize trends in age and crime because when making laws, or trying to prevent future crimes from happening (especially among the youth). It can also show how societal changes affect crime; the economy, politics and even natural disasters affect crime rates. One interesting trend to observe is that 18-29 year olds seem to have the highest arrest rate, including crimes for drug abuse, DUIs and assaults. Data can be found at: FBI: UCR Crime Rates
